Why This Website Exists

Three commitments we build on.

Theology is too important to be settled by slogans. These are the commitments that shape everything published here.

I.

Scripture Before System

Every doctrine must be defensible from the text itself — not simply assumed because it fits a theological system.

II.

Argument, Not Attack

We examine positions on their own terms, and respond with exegesis, history, and documentation — not polemics.

III.

Open to Examination

Sources and biblical texts are laid out plainly, so you can study the evidence and judge for yourself.

What Is the Gospel?

The good news, in brief.

The gospel is the good news that God, in His love, sent His Son, Jesus Christ, to save sinners. Jesus lived the perfect life we could not live, and died in our place on the cross to atone for sin.

He rose from the dead on the third day, conquering death itself. All who turn from sin and place their trust in Christ alone are forgiven and made new by God's grace.

This is not something we earn or achieve — it is a gift received through faith.

Responding to Common Calvinist Claims

Claim. Response.

A sample of common arguments, weighed against the texts they are said to rest on.

Claim01

"The spiritually dead cannot believe."

Response

Scripture uses "death" as a picture of serious separation from God, not an inability to respond — otherwise no call to faith or repentance addressed to the lost would make sense.

Claim02

"John 6 teaches Calvinism."

Response

The passage describes the Father's drawing toward the Son, but does not say this drawing is irresistible for everyone it touches — the wider context shows some who were drawn still turned away.

Claim03

"Romans 9 teaches double predestination."

Response

The context of Romans 9 deals primarily with God's right to shape the role of nations and His unfolding plan of redemption — not the eternal fate of individuals fixed apart from faith.
