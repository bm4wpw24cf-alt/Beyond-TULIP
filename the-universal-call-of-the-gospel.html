The Gospel · VII

The Universal Call of the Gospel

The gospel is good news for the whole world.

From the beginning of Jesus' ministry to the preaching of the apostles, the message of salvation was proclaimed openly and sincerely to all people. The New Testament never presents the gospel as an invitation reserved for a select few. Instead, it reveals God's universal call for all people to repent, believe, and receive eternal life through Jesus Christ.

Jesus Invited Everyone

Jesus' public ministry was marked by open invitations.

He called sinners to repentance (Mark 1:15).

He invited the weary and burdened to come to Him (Matthew 11:28–30).

He declared that whoever believes in Him will have eternal life (John 3:16).

His ministry consistently demonstrated that God's invitation extends to people from every background—Jews and Gentiles, the righteous and the unrighteous, the wealthy and the poor.

The Great Commission

After His resurrection, Jesus commissioned His disciples:

"Go into all the world and proclaim the gospel to the whole creation."Mark 16:15

Similarly, He commanded them to make disciples of all nations (Matthew 28:18–20).

The church was never instructed to determine who might be among God's people before preaching. Instead, the gospel is to be proclaimed to everyone because Christ is the Savior of the world.

God Calls Everyone to Repent

The apostles faithfully continued Jesus' mission.

Paul declared that God "commands all people everywhere to repent" (Acts 17:30).

This universal command reflects God's universal call.

God does not merely invite one nation or one group. His message is directed to every person.

Wherever the gospel is preached, God is calling people to respond in repentance and faith.

The Gospel Is Offered to Everyone

The invitation of the gospel is genuine.

Jesus proclaimed:

"Whoever comes to Me I will never cast out."John 6:37

The final invitation of Scripture echoes the same truth:

"Let the one who is thirsty come; let the one who desires take the water of life without price."Revelation 22:17

These invitations are not limited by ethnicity, social status, or past failures.

Anyone may come to Christ.

God's Desire for Humanity

The New Testament reveals God's compassionate heart toward the world.

God desires all people to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth (1 Timothy 2:3–6).

He is patient, not wishing that any should perish but that all should come to repentance (2 Peter 3:9).

These passages reveal God's gracious disposition toward humanity and explain why the gospel is proclaimed without distinction.

Why Do Some Reject the Gospel?

Although God's invitation is universal, not everyone responds in faith.

Some refuse to repent. Some harden their hearts. Some love darkness rather than light (John 3:19–20).

The tragedy is not that the invitation was withheld, but that it was rejected.

The New Testament consistently places responsibility on those who refuse the gospel.

Faith Is the Required Response

The gospel calls for a response.

People are not saved merely by hearing the message.

They are called to repent, believe in Jesus Christ, confess Him as Lord, and be baptized as the biblical expression of faith (Mark 1:15; Acts 2:38; Romans 10:9–10).

Salvation is entirely by God's grace, yet His grace calls for a response of obedient faith.

The Church's Mission

Because the gospel is for everyone, the church must proclaim it to everyone.

No one is beyond God's mercy. No one is too sinful to come to Christ. No one should be excluded from hearing the good news.

The church's mission is not to determine who may come, but to faithfully proclaim Christ to the whole world.

Conclusion

The universal call of the gospel reflects the universal love of God and the universal scope of Christ's saving work.

God calls all people everywhere to repent.

Jesus invites everyone to come.

The Holy Spirit bears witness through the preaching of the gospel.

Everyone who comes to Christ in faith will be welcomed, forgiven, and given eternal life.

The church therefore proclaims the gospel with confidence to every person, trusting God's promise:

"Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord shall be saved."Romans 10:13
