Texts Often Used in the Debate

These studies revisit some of the passages most frequently cited in debates over election, inability, grace, repentance, and God's saving purpose, reading each text carefully in its own context.

Introduction

Some biblical passages are quoted so often in theological debate that their context can be overshadowed by the system people bring to them. This section gathers those texts in one place and studies them inductively, asking what each passage actually says before asking how it fits a theological framework.

The goal is not to avoid doctrine, but to let doctrine be shaped by Scripture rather than by assumptions imported into the text.

How These Studies Work

  • The immediate literary context is examined before doctrinal conclusions are drawn.
  • Repeated theological claims are tested against the passage itself, not simply repeated by tradition.
  • Related texts are compared so no single verse is made to carry more than Scripture allows.
  • The aim is clarity, fairness, and fidelity to the biblical text.
