Assurance of Salvation

One of the greatest blessings of the gospel is that believers can have assurance of salvation.

Christian assurance is not based on feelings, personal performance, or religious achievements. It is grounded in God's promises, the finished work of Jesus Christ, and the witness of the Holy Spirit.

Our Assurance Begins with Christ

Our confidence rests on what Jesus has accomplished.

Christ alone:

  • Lived a sinless life.
  • Died for our sins.
  • Rose from the dead.
  • Intercedes for believers.
  • Will never fail His promises.

Salvation rests on Him—not on us (Hebrews 7:25; Romans 8:34).

God's Promises Never Fail

God's promises are the foundation of our assurance.

Jesus promises:

  • Whoever believes has eternal life (John 3:16).
  • Whoever hears His word and believes has passed from death to life (John 5:24).
  • Whoever comes to Him will never be cast out (John 6:37).

John wrote his first letter so believers could know they have eternal life (1 John 5:11–13).

Salvation Is by Grace

Salvation is God's gift. It is:

  • By grace.
  • Through faith.
  • Not earned by works (Ephesians 2:8–9).

Our assurance is therefore based on God's grace, not our own perfection.

When believers sin, they are called to confess their sins, trusting God's faithfulness to forgive and cleanse them (1 John 1:9).

The Holy Spirit Gives Assurance

Every believer receives the Holy Spirit. The Holy Spirit:

  • Bears witness that we are God's children.
  • Produces spiritual fruit.
  • Convicts us of sin.
  • Strengthens our faith.
  • Points us to Christ (Romans 8:15–16; Galatians 5:22–23).

His presence confirms God's work within us.

A Changed Life Gives Evidence

Good works do not save us, but they demonstrate that our faith is genuine.

A growing Christian life is characterized by:

  • Love for God.
  • Love for others.
  • Obedience to Christ.
  • Desire for holiness.
  • Perseverance in faith.

Jesus said a tree is known by its fruit (Matthew 7:16–20).

John likewise teaches that those who know God increasingly obey Him and love one another (1 John 2:3–6; 1 John 3:14).

When Believers Doubt

Even sincere Christians sometimes struggle with doubt. Doubt may arise because of:

  • Personal failure.
  • Temptation.
  • Suffering.
  • Fear.
  • Difficult circumstances.

When doubts come, believers should return to:

  • God's promises.
  • The finished work of Christ.
  • Prayer.
  • Scripture.
  • Fellowship with other believers.

Our feelings change, but God's Word does not.

Continue in Christ

The New Testament encourages believers to continue in faith.

Jesus calls His followers to abide in Him (John 15:4–5).

The apostles encourage believers to:

  • Continue in the faith (Colossians 1:21–23).
  • Hold firmly to Christ (Hebrews 3:14).
  • Grow in grace (2 Peter 3:18).

Perseverance does not earn salvation—it is the evidence of a living faith.

Christ Is Faithful

Our confidence ultimately rests in Jesus Christ. He is:

  • Our Savior.
  • Our Good Shepherd.
  • Our High Priest.
  • Our Advocate before the Father (1 John 2:1–2).
  • Able to save completely those who come to God through Him (Hebrews 7:25).

Our hope rests in His faithfulness, not our own.

Conclusion

The assurance of salvation is found in Jesus Christ.

Our confidence rests on:

  • God's promises.
  • Christ's finished work.
  • The witness of the Holy Spirit.
  • A life being transformed by God's grace.

Believers do not need to live in constant fear or uncertainty.

Our hope is not found in our feelings or performance, but in the faithful Savior who loved us, gave Himself for us, and keeps His promises.

"These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life." (1 John 5:13)
