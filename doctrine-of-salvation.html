Doctrine of Salvation

What does the Bible teach about salvation? This section examines the major doctrines of salvation through careful study of Scripture, allowing the biblical text to shape our theology.

Introduction

Salvation stands at the very heart of the Christian faith. Throughout church history, believers have debated questions concerning election, grace, faith, repentance, regeneration, justification, sanctification, perseverance, and eternal security. While theological systems often seek to organize these doctrines, our goal is to begin with Scripture itself.

This section is not designed to defend a particular theological tradition. Instead, it seeks to examine each doctrine in its biblical context, comparing passages carefully and allowing the whole counsel of God to speak.

Our Approach

Every article in this section follows the same principles:

  • Scripture is the highest authority.
  • Immediate context is examined first.
  • Historical and literary context are considered.
  • Original-language observations are included when helpful.
  • Alternative interpretations are presented fairly.
  • Conclusions are based on the biblical evidence.

Purpose

The purpose of this section is to encourage careful Bible study rather than dependence on theological traditions. Our desire is to help readers understand what Scripture teaches about God's work of salvation from beginning to end.
