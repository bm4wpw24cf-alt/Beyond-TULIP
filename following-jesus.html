The Gospel · IX

Following Jesus

The gospel does not end with conversion—it begins there.

Jesus did not simply call people to believe certain truths about Him. He called them to follow Him. Faith in Christ is the beginning of a lifelong relationship of love, trust, obedience, and transformation.

Salvation is God's gift of grace. Discipleship is the lifelong response of those who have received that gift.

Jesus Calls Us to Follow Him

Throughout the Gospels, Jesus repeatedly invited people with two simple words: "Follow Me."

  • Following Jesus means more than admiring His teachings.
  • It means entrusting our lives to Him as Lord.
  • It means learning to live according to His will (Matthew 4:19; John 10:27).

A disciple is not merely someone who knows about Jesus but someone who walks with Him.

Deny Yourself and Take Up Your Cross

Jesus taught that following Him requires commitment. He said that anyone who would come after Him must deny himself, take up his cross daily, and follow Him (Luke 9:23).

This does not mean earning salvation through sacrifice. Salvation is a free gift of God's grace. Rather, it means placing Christ above every other loyalty and allowing Him to shape every area of life.

Abide in Christ

The Christian life is not lived by human strength.

"Apart from Me you can do nothing."John 15:5
  • To follow Christ is to remain in Him.
  • We depend on Him through prayer, His Word, and the work of the Holy Spirit.
  • As branches draw life from the vine, believers receive spiritual life from Christ.

Obedience Is the Fruit of Love

"If you love Me, you will keep My commandments."John 14:15
  • Obedience does not earn God's love.
  • It is the natural response of those who have already experienced His grace.
  • A transformed heart produces a transformed life.

Growing to Become Like Christ

God's purpose is not merely to forgive sinners but to transform them into the likeness of His Son (Romans 8:29). This lifelong process is often called sanctification.

  • The Holy Spirit works within believers, producing Christlike character: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control (Galatians 5:22–23).
  • Spiritual growth is gradual, but God is faithful to complete the work He has begun (Philippians 1:6).

Loving God and Loving Others

Jesus summarized God's commandments in two great commands:

  • Love God with all your heart.
  • Love your neighbor as yourself (Matthew 22:37–40).

The Christian life is therefore marked not only by sound doctrine but also by genuine love. Jesus taught that the world would recognize His disciples by their love for one another (John 13:34–35).

Making Disciples

Following Jesus includes helping others follow Him.

  • Before returning to heaven, Jesus commanded His followers to make disciples of all nations.
  • Baptizing them and teaching them to obey everything He had commanded (Matthew 28:18–20).
  • Every Christian is called to bear witness to Christ through both words and actions.

Persevering in Faith

The Christian life includes trials, suffering, and spiritual warfare. Jesus never promised an easy path, but He promised His presence.

  • Believers are called to remain faithful, trusting Christ through every season of life (John 16:33; Hebrews 12:1–2).
  • Our confidence rests not in our own strength but in God's sustaining grace.

The Goal of Discipleship

The ultimate goal of following Jesus is not merely to gain knowledge but to know Him personally.

  • As we walk with Christ, our lives increasingly reflect His character, His compassion, and His holiness.
  • One day, faith will become sight, and those who belong to Christ will be with Him forever (1 John 3:2; Revelation 21:3–4).

Conclusion

Following Jesus is the lifelong journey of every believer:

  • It begins with repentance and faith.
  • It continues through daily dependence upon Christ.
  • It is marked by love, obedience, holiness, and perseverance.

The Christian life is not about earning God's favor. It is about walking with the One who loved us, gave Himself for us, and calls us to follow Him.

As Jesus said:

"My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me."John 10:27
