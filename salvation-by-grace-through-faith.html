The Gospel · III

Salvation by Grace Through Faith

Salvation is God's gracious gift to sinners. It cannot be earned, deserved, or purchased. From beginning to end, salvation is made possible by God's grace and is received through faith in Jesus Christ.

What Is Grace?

Grace is God's unmerited favor toward undeserving sinners. It is His loving initiative to save those who could never save themselves.

God was under no obligation to rescue humanity. Yet, because of His love and mercy, He sent His Son to accomplish our redemption (John 3:16; Titus 3:4–7).

Grace does not mean that sin is ignored. Rather, it means that God Himself has provided the remedy for sin through Jesus Christ.

Salvation Is Not Earned

The Bible consistently teaches that no one can earn salvation through good works, religious ceremonies, or personal righteousness.

No amount of morality or human effort can remove guilt or make us acceptable before a holy God (Romans 3:20; Titus 3:5).

If salvation could be earned, it would no longer be a gift.

Faith Receives What Grace Provides

While salvation originates in God's grace, it is received through faith.

Faith is not a work that earns salvation. It is trusting in Jesus Christ alone—relying on His finished work rather than on ourselves.

Biblical faith includes believing the truth about Christ, placing our confidence in Him, and responding to His call with repentance and obedience.

The Object of Saving Faith

Saving faith is not faith in ourselves, our church, our baptism, or our religious performance.

The object of saving faith is Jesus Christ.

He alone lived a sinless life, died for our sins, rose from the dead, and now offers forgiveness and eternal life to everyone who believes in Him (Acts 4:12).

Grace Produces a Transformed Life

Grace does not merely forgive sinners—it transforms them.

Those who trust in Christ are given new life, reconciled to God, adopted into His family, and empowered by the Holy Spirit to grow in holiness.

Good works do not earn salvation, but they are the natural fruit of a life that has been changed by God's grace (Ephesians 2:10; James 2:17).

The Invitation of the Gospel

The gospel is proclaimed to all people.

God calls everyone everywhere to repent and believe the good news (Acts 17:30; Mark 1:15).

Everyone who places their faith in Jesus Christ receives the forgiveness of sins and the gift of eternal life.

Assurance in Christ

The believer's confidence rests not in personal achievement but in Christ Himself.

Our hope is grounded in His finished work on the cross, His victorious resurrection, and His faithful promises.

Salvation is secure because it depends on the faithfulness of the Savior, not the perfection of the sinner.

Conclusion

Salvation is entirely the work of God's grace.

Jesus Christ accomplished everything necessary for our redemption through His life, death, and resurrection.

This gift cannot be earned by human effort. It is freely offered by God's grace and received through faith in Jesus Christ.

To God alone belongs all the glory.
