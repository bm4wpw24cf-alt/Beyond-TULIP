# 18. Hermeneutics Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the principles of biblical interpretation used throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that Scripture is interpreted consistently, contextually, and faithfully according to sound hermeneutical principles.

---

## Primary Principle

The meaning of Scripture is determined by the intent of the inspired biblical authors as expressed through the text.

Interpretation seeks to discover meaning rather than create it.

---

## Authorial Intent

The primary objective of interpretation is to understand what the biblical author intended to communicate to the original audience.

Modern application should follow original meaning.

---

## Context Governs Meaning

Every passage should be interpreted within:

- Immediate context
- Literary context
- Historical context
- Canonical context

Context always has priority over isolated wording.

---

## Scripture Interprets Scripture

Clear passages should help explain more difficult passages.

No interpretation should contradict the overall teaching of Scripture.

---

## Genre Awareness

Interpret each passage according to its literary genre.

Examples include:

- Narrative
- Law
- Poetry
- Wisdom
- Prophecy
- Gospel
- Epistle
- Apocalypse

Genre influences interpretation.

---

## Progressive Revelation

God's revelation unfolds progressively throughout Scripture.

Later revelation may clarify earlier revelation without changing its original meaning.

---

## Literal and Figurative Language

Interpret language according to the author's intended use.

Recognize:

- Metaphor
- Simile
- Hyperbole
- Symbolism
- Irony
- Personification

Literal interpretation does not require wooden literalism.

---

## Difficult Passages

When passages appear difficult:

- Study the immediate context.
- Compare parallel passages.
- Examine original languages where relevant.
- Present major interpretations fairly.
- Avoid premature harmonization.

Difficulty should encourage careful study rather than speculation.

---

## Theological Systems

Hermeneutics should govern theology.

Theological systems should not govern hermeneutics.

Interpretation comes before doctrinal synthesis.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Follow sound hermeneutical principles.
- Respect context.
- Distinguish observation from interpretation.
- Explain interpretive decisions.
- Avoid reading later theology into the text.

---

## Final Principle

Faithful theology begins with faithful interpretation.

> **Interpret Scripture according to its context before drawing theological conclusions.**
