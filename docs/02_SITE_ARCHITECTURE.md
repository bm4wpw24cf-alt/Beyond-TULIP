# 02. Site Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines the structure of the Beyond TULIP website.

Its purpose is to ensure that the project remains organized, scalable, consistent, and easy to maintain as it grows.

The architecture is designed to support hundreds of articles without becoming difficult to navigate or maintain.

## Navigation Strategy

The navigation system is designed to remain simple, consistent, and intuitive regardless of how large the project becomes.

Primary navigation should contain only the main sections of the website and should rarely change.

Secondary navigation may be used within individual sections to organize related content.

Breadcrumb navigation should be implemented on article pages to help users understand where they are within the site hierarchy.

Navigation should always prioritize readability over complexity.

---

## URL Structure

URLs should be descriptive, human-readable, and remain stable over time.

Examples:

/articles/topics/salvation/

/articles/topics/election/

/articles/series/understanding-tulip/

/articles/series/responding-to-calvinist-claims/

/bible/new-testament/john/

/bible/old-testament/isaiah/

/diagrams/salvation/

URLs should avoid unnecessary parameters and remain consistent throughout the project.

---

## Internal Linking Strategy

Every article should connect naturally to related resources throughout the website.

Whenever appropriate, articles should include links to:

- Related articles
- Bible commentary
- Relevant diagrams
- Related article series
- Frequently asked questions
- External resources when appropriate

Internal linking should help readers continue studying the subject without unnecessary navigation.

---

## Scalability

The architecture is designed to support long-term growth.

Future expansion should not require restructuring the website.

New content should normally be added within existing categories rather than creating new top-level sections.

The architecture should comfortably support:

- Hundreds of theological articles
- Complete Bible commentary
- Large collections of diagrams
- Historical resources
- Apologetics materials
- Future educational resources

---

## Future Expansion

The architecture should allow new sections to be introduced without disrupting the existing structure.

Possible future additions include:

- Church History
- Book Reviews
- Creeds & Confessions
- Theology Dictionary
- Biblical Timelines
- Maps
- Video Library
- Audio Library
- Download Center

These additions should integrate naturally into the existing navigation system.

---

## Architectural Principles

All future development should follow these principles:

- Simplicity before complexity.
- Consistency before innovation.
- Reuse existing structures whenever possible.
- Preserve a stable navigation system.
- Maintain accessibility and readability.
- Support AI-assisted development.
- Build for long-term maintainability.
- Keep Scripture at the center of the project's purpose.

---

## Conclusion

The architecture of Beyond TULIP is intended to provide a stable foundation for a growing theological library.

Every structural decision should support the long-term mission of presenting biblical truth clearly, organizing theological knowledge effectively, and helping readers study Scripture with confidence.
