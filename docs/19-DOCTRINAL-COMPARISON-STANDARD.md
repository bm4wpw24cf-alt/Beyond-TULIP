# 19. Doctrinal Comparison Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standard for comparing theological doctrines throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that doctrinal comparisons are accurate, balanced, transparent, and grounded primarily in Scripture.

---

## Primary Principle

The purpose of comparison is understanding before evaluation.

Every doctrinal position should be represented fairly before its strengths and weaknesses are assessed.

---

## Comparison Method

Every comparison should normally follow this sequence:

1. Define the doctrine.
2. Explain its historical development.
3. Present its primary biblical arguments.
4. Present its principal theological arguments.
5. Present major criticisms.
6. Evaluate the evidence.
7. Draw carefully supported conclusions.

---

## Equal Standards

Every doctrinal position should be evaluated using the same criteria.

The AI should avoid:

- Double standards.
- Unequal evidence requirements.
- Preferential treatment.

---

## Common Ground

Begin by identifying areas of agreement.

Shared beliefs should be acknowledged before discussing disagreements.

---

## Areas of Difference

Differences should be described accurately.

The AI should explain:

- What the disagreement is.
- Why it exists.
- Which biblical passages are central.
- How each position interprets those passages.

---

## Primary Sources

Whenever possible, compare positions using their own primary sources.

Avoid relying solely on critics of a theological position.

---

## Evaluation

Evaluation should consider:

- Biblical evidence
- Historical evidence
- Literary context
- Original languages
- Logical consistency
- Theological coherence

---

## Tone

Comparisons should remain:

- Fair
- Respectful
- Evidence-based
- Objective
- Charitable

The goal is understanding rather than winning arguments.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Compare accurately.
- Explain differences clearly.
- Avoid caricatures.
- Represent each position fairly.
- Support conclusions with evidence.

---

## Final Principle

A fair comparison strengthens understanding, even where disagreement remains.

> **Compare honestly. Evaluate fairly. Let Scripture remain the final authority.**
