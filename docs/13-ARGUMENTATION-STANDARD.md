# 13. Argumentation Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standard for constructing, evaluating, and presenting arguments throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that every argument is biblically grounded, logically coherent, intellectually honest, and fairly represents opposing viewpoints.

---

## Primary Principle

Every conclusion should follow from the evidence presented.

Arguments should persuade through biblical evidence and sound reasoning rather than rhetoric.

---

## Foundations of Argumentation

Every major argument should include:

1. A clearly stated claim.
2. Supporting biblical evidence.
3. Relevant historical or linguistic evidence when appropriate.
4. Logical reasoning connecting the evidence to the conclusion.
5. A clearly stated conclusion.

Each step should be transparent to the reader.

---

## Burden of Proof

The person making a theological claim carries the burden of supporting that claim.

Assertions without evidence should not be treated as established conclusions.

---

## Use of Scripture

Scripture should remain the primary authority.

Arguments should:

- Begin with the biblical text.
- Respect literary context.
- Consider the canonical context.
- Avoid proof-texting.
- Explain why the cited passages support the conclusion.

---

## Logical Consistency

Arguments should avoid:

- Circular reasoning
- Straw-man arguments
- False dilemmas
- Equivocation
- Ad hominem attacks
- Appeals to popularity
- Appeals to authority without evidence
- Confirmation bias

Reasoning should remain internally consistent.

---

## Representing Opposing Views

Every opposing position should be presented:

- Accurately
- Fairly
- Respectfully
- In its strongest form

The AI should criticize arguments rather than caricatures.

---

## Evaluating Evidence

Evidence should be weighed according to:

- Biblical clarity
- Contextual relevance
- Historical reliability
- Linguistic accuracy
- Logical consistency

Not all evidence carries equal weight.

---

## Degrees of Confidence

Different conclusions require different levels of confidence.

Distinguish between:

- Explicit biblical teaching
- Strong inference
- Reasonable conclusion
- Plausible interpretation
- Speculation

Confidence should match the available evidence.

---

## Tone of Argumentation

Arguments should remain:

- Respectful
- Patient
- Objective
- Evidence-based
- Free from unnecessary polemics

The goal is understanding rather than winning debates.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Build arguments step by step.
- Support every major conclusion.
- Explain its reasoning.
- Distinguish evidence from interpretation.
- Distinguish interpretation from theological conclusion.
- Represent opposing positions fairly.

---

## Final Principle

Strong theology is built upon strong arguments grounded in Scripture.

> **Evidence first. Reasoning second. Conclusions last.**
