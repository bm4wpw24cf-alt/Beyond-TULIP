# 15. Theological Terminology Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standards for using theological terminology throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that theological terms are used consistently, accurately, and according to their historical and biblical meanings.

---

## Primary Principle

Clear theology requires clear definitions.

No important theological term should be assumed to have a universally accepted meaning.

---

## Define Before Discussing

Before evaluating a theological concept, define it clearly.

Definitions should:

- Be historically accurate.
- Reflect common scholarly usage.
- Distinguish between biblical terminology and theological terminology.
- Avoid ambiguity.

---

## Biblical Terms

Whenever possible:

- Begin with the biblical vocabulary.
- Explain the original Hebrew, Aramaic, or Greek term when relevant.
- Show how the biblical authors use the term.

Biblical usage takes priority over later theological usage.

---

## Historical Development

Many theological terms developed after the completion of Scripture.

When appropriate, explain:

- Historical origin
- Development through church history
- Major theological usage
- Modern usage

---

## Multiple Definitions

Some theological terms have multiple accepted meanings.

Examples include:

- Election
- Predestination
- Regeneration
- Grace
- Justification
- Sanctification
- Atonement

When multiple definitions exist:

- Identify them clearly.
- Explain the differences.
- Avoid assuming only one definition exists.

---

## Technical Vocabulary

Use technical theological terms only when they improve clarity.

Whenever technical language is introduced:

- Define it.
- Explain it simply.
- Use it consistently.

---

## Consistency

Once a term has been defined within an article, use that definition consistently.

Avoid shifting meanings during an argument.

---

## Avoid Equivocation

The AI should never argue by changing the meaning of a theological term.

Definitions should remain stable throughout the discussion.

---

## Historical Labels

Labels such as:

- Calvinist
- Arminian
- Lutheran
- Reformed
- Baptist
- Catholic
- Orthodox

should be used carefully.

Whenever possible:

- Define the label.
- Explain its historical meaning.
- Avoid stereotypes.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Define important theological terms.
- Distinguish biblical language from systematic theology.
- Explain historical development.
- Maintain consistent terminology.
- Avoid ambiguous language.

---

## Final Principle

Good theology begins with clear definitions.

> **Define carefully. Use consistently. Communicate clearly.**
