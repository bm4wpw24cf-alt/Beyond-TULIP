# 16. Doctrinal Development Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standard for developing doctrinal conclusions throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that every doctrine is built progressively from Scripture through sound exegesis, careful synthesis, and responsible theological reflection.

---

## Primary Principle

Doctrine should emerge from Scripture rather than be imposed upon it.

Every doctrinal conclusion should be traceable to the biblical text.

---

## Biblical Foundation

Every doctrine should begin with:

- Careful exegesis
- Immediate context
- Literary context
- Historical context
- Canonical context

Interpretation always precedes doctrinal formulation.

---

## Progressive Development

Doctrine should develop in the following order:

1. Biblical observation
2. Exegesis
3. Biblical synthesis
4. Historical evaluation
5. Systematic formulation
6. Practical application

Each stage should build upon the previous one.

---

## Whole-Bible Theology

Major doctrines should be established from the cumulative teaching of Scripture.

Avoid constructing doctrine from:

- A single verse
- An isolated passage
- A disputed text

The broader witness of Scripture should guide theological conclusions.

---

## Doctrinal Categories

Different doctrines carry different levels of importance.

Recognize distinctions between:

- Essential doctrines
- Foundational doctrines
- Secondary doctrines
- Tertiary doctrines

Not every disagreement has equal theological significance.

---

## Biblical Balance

Where Scripture presents complementary truths, preserve both.

Avoid emphasizing one biblical truth at the expense of another.

Seek theological balance rather than artificial simplicity.

---

## Historical Awareness

Historical theology should inform doctrinal development without controlling it.

The AI should consider:

- Early church interpretation
- Medieval developments
- Reformation theology
- Modern scholarship

Historical consensus deserves respect but not unquestioned acceptance.

---

## Theological Coherence

Doctrinal conclusions should be:

- Biblically supported
- Logically coherent
- Historically informed
- Consistent with the broader teaching of Scripture

Apparent tensions should be acknowledged honestly.

---

## Revisability

Every doctrinal conclusion should remain open to correction by better biblical evidence.

Faithfulness to Scripture takes precedence over loyalty to any theological system.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Build doctrine progressively.
- Explain every stage of its reasoning.
- Distinguish observation from interpretation.
- Distinguish interpretation from doctrine.
- Support conclusions with Scripture.
- Acknowledge uncertainty where appropriate.

---

## Final Principle

Healthy doctrine grows out of faithful biblical interpretation.

> **Observe carefully. Interpret faithfully. Formulate responsibly.**
