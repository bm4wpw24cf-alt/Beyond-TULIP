# 08. Bible Article Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standard for writing biblical and theological articles throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that every article is accurate, consistent, readable, well-structured, and firmly grounded in Scripture.

The Bible Study Standard defines **how Scripture is studied**. This document defines **how the results of that study are presented**.

---

## Primary Objective

Every article should help the reader:

- Understand the biblical text.
- Understand the historical and literary context.
- Follow the exegetical reasoning.
- Evaluate differing interpretations fairly.
- Reach conclusions based on Scripture rather than theological tradition.

The goal is explanation before persuasion.

---

## General Structure

A typical article should follow this sequence.

### 1. Title

The title should clearly describe the subject without unnecessary sensationalism.

Examples:

- What Does Romans 9 Teach?
- Understanding Election in Ephesians 1
- Did Jesus Die for the Whole World?

---

### 2. Introduction

Introduce:

- The biblical question
- Why it matters
- What the article will examine

Avoid presenting conclusions before the evidence.

---

### 3. Historical Background

When relevant, explain:

- Author
- Audience
- Historical setting
- Cultural background
- Covenant context

---

### 4. Literary Context

Explain how the passage fits within:

- The paragraph
- The chapter
- The book
- The wider biblical argument

---

### 5. Exegetical Analysis

This should be the largest section of the article.

Discuss:

- Grammar
- Key words
- Syntax
- Immediate context
- Biblical flow of thought
- Relevant cross-references

Support every conclusion from the text.

---

### 6. Original Language Notes

Include Hebrew, Aramaic, or Greek only when they materially clarify the passage.

Avoid unnecessary technical discussion.

---

### 7. Cross-References

Use Scripture to interpret Scripture.

Cross-references should strengthen the argument rather than simply increase the number of references.

---

### 8. Alternative Interpretations

Present the strongest major interpretations fairly.

For each interpretation:

- Explain it accurately.
- Present its supporting arguments.
- Evaluate it from the biblical text.

Avoid straw-man arguments.

Every major view should be presented as its proponents would recognize it.

---

### 9. Conclusion

Summarize the evidence.

Explain why the preferred interpretation best fits the biblical data.

Avoid overstating certainty where genuine uncertainty remains.

---

### 10. Practical Significance

Where appropriate, explain how the passage informs:

- Christian doctrine
- Christian living
- Worship
- Evangelism
- Church life

Application should arise naturally from the text.

---

## Writing Style

Articles should be:

- Biblically focused
- Clear
- Logical
- Well organized
- Academically responsible
- Readable for ordinary Christians
- Respectful toward differing views

Avoid unnecessary jargon.

---

## Citation Practice

Whenever possible:

- Quote Scripture accurately.
- Cite biblical references clearly.
- Distinguish biblical observation from theological interpretation.
- Distinguish historical facts from theological conclusions.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Explain before concluding.
- Follow the biblical evidence step by step.
- Present competing interpretations fairly.
- Avoid theological bias during exegesis.
- Write with clarity, precision, and humility.
- Support every major conclusion with biblical evidence.
- Distinguish clearly between biblical text, interpretation, and theological conclusion.

---

## Final Principle

Every article should enable readers to understand not only **what** conclusion is reached, but also **why** that conclusion follows from Scripture.

> **Clear reasoning. Faithful exegesis. Biblical conclusions.**
