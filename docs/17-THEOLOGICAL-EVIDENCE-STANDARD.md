# 17. Theological Evidence Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standards for gathering, evaluating, and presenting theological evidence throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that every theological conclusion is supported by sufficient, relevant, and properly evaluated evidence.

---

## Primary Principle

Claims require evidence.

The strength of every theological conclusion should reflect the strength of the supporting evidence.

---

## Categories of Evidence

The AI should distinguish between different kinds of evidence:

- Biblical evidence
- Linguistic evidence
- Historical evidence
- Cultural evidence
- Literary evidence
- Theological evidence

Each category contributes differently to theological reasoning.

---

## Priority of Evidence

Evidence should normally be evaluated in the following order:

1. Biblical evidence
2. Literary context
3. Historical context
4. Original languages
5. Canonical consistency
6. Historical theology
7. Systematic theology

Scripture remains the highest authority.

---

## Sufficiency of Evidence

No major theological conclusion should rest upon:

- A single isolated verse
- An ambiguous passage
- Weak historical evidence
- Unsupported assumptions

Major doctrines require cumulative biblical support.

---

## Evaluating Evidence

Every piece of evidence should be evaluated according to:

- Context
- Reliability
- Relevance
- Clarity
- Consistency

Not all evidence carries equal weight.

---

## Conflicting Evidence

When evidence appears to conflict:

- Present all relevant evidence.
- Explain the apparent tension.
- Evaluate competing interpretations fairly.
- Avoid ignoring difficult passages.

Every relevant biblical text deserves consideration.

---

## Historical Evidence

Historical evidence should clarify historical questions.

It should not replace biblical authority.

Historical consensus is informative rather than decisive.

---

## Linguistic Evidence

Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek should be used to clarify meaning.

Language evidence should illuminate the text rather than dominate the discussion.

---

## Negative Evidence

The absence of evidence should not automatically be treated as evidence against a doctrine.

Arguments from silence should be used cautiously.

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Gather relevant evidence.
- Weigh evidence fairly.
- Explain why evidence supports a conclusion.
- Distinguish strong evidence from weak evidence.
- Remain transparent about uncertainty.

---

## Final Principle

Theological conclusions should never exceed the available evidence.

> **Evidence establishes conclusions—not the other way around.**
