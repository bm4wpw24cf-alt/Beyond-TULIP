# 11. Scripture Citation Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the standards for quoting, referencing, and explaining Scripture throughout the Beyond TULIP project.

Its purpose is to ensure that biblical citations are accurate, consistent, transparent, and easy for readers to verify.

---

## Primary Principle

Scripture should always be represented accurately and in its proper context.

Biblical references exist to support the argument, not to replace it.

---

## Accuracy

The AI should:

- Quote Scripture accurately.
- Preserve the intended meaning of the passage.
- Avoid altering biblical wording to strengthen an argument.
- Clearly distinguish quotations from commentary.

---

## Context

Every Scripture citation should be interpreted within:

- Its immediate context
- The surrounding paragraph
- The chapter
- The book
- The wider biblical canon

Verses should never be isolated from their literary context.

---

## Citation Format

Scripture references should be:

- Accurate
- Consistent
- Easy to locate
- Clearly distinguished from commentary

Examples:

- John 3:16
- Romans 8:28–30
- Ephesians 2:8–10

---

## Multiple References

When citing several passages:

- Present them in a logical order.
- Group closely related references together.
- Avoid overwhelming the reader with excessive lists.

Quality is more valuable than quantity.

---

## Old and New Testament

Whenever appropriate:

- Demonstrate how the Old Testament informs the New Testament.
- Explain New Testament use of Old Testament passages.
- Distinguish direct quotations from allusions.

---

## Original Languages

Original language observations should be included only when they materially improve understanding.

Avoid unnecessary technical discussion.

Language notes should clarify rather than impress.

---

## Cross-References

Cross-references should:

- Explain Scripture with Scripture.
- Support the current discussion.
- Avoid superficial word associations.

Only meaningful parallels should be cited.

---

## Difficult Passages

When a passage is disputed:

- Explain why.
- Present the principal interpretations fairly.
- Evaluate them from the biblical evidence.
- State the preferred interpretation with supporting reasons.

---

## Scripture and Doctrine

Doctrinal conclusions should arise from the cumulative witness of Scripture.

No major doctrine should depend entirely upon:

- One isolated verse
- An obscure passage
- A disputed textual variant

---

## AI Expectations

The AI should:

- Quote Scripture accurately.
- Respect literary context.
- Explain rather than merely cite.
- Use cross-references responsibly.
- Support doctrine with the broader teaching of Scripture.
- Distinguish biblical text from theological interpretation.

---

## Final Principle

Every Scripture citation should help readers understand the biblical text more clearly.

> **Quote accurately. Interpret faithfully. Apply responsibly.**
