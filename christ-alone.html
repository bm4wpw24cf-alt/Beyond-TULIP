The Gospel · XII

Christ Alone

At the heart of the Christian faith stands one person: Jesus Christ.

Christianity is not founded upon a philosophy, a moral code, or a theological system. It is founded upon the person and work of Jesus Christ.

The gospel is the good news of who He is, what He has accomplished, and what He freely offers to all who believe.

Jesus Is the Only Savior

The Bible declares that salvation is found in Christ alone.

Jesus alone:

  • Lived a sinless life.
  • Fulfilled God's Law perfectly.
  • Died for our sins.
  • Rose from the dead.
  • Conquered sin and death.
  • Reigns forever as Lord.

There is no other Savior, no other Mediator between God and humanity, and no other name by which we must be saved (Acts 4:12; 1 Timothy 2:5–6).

Christ Alone Accomplished Our Salvation

Everything necessary for our salvation was accomplished by Jesus Christ.

Through His death:

  • He bore our sins.
  • He made atonement.
  • He satisfied God's justice.
  • He reconciled sinners to God.

Through His resurrection:

  • He conquered death.
  • He defeated the grave.
  • He gives eternal life to everyone who believes.

Nothing can be added to His finished work (Hebrews 10:10–14).

Salvation Is Found in Christ Alone

Every blessing of salvation is found in Jesus Christ.

Through Him believers receive:

  • Forgiveness of sins.
  • Justification.
  • Reconciliation with God.
  • Adoption into God's family.
  • The gift of the Holy Spirit.
  • Eternal life.
  • The hope of resurrection.

Faith does not earn these blessings. Faith simply receives what God has provided through His Son (Ephesians 2:8–9; Ephesians 1:3–14).

Christ Is the Center of Scripture

From Genesis to Revelation, the Bible points to Jesus Christ.

  • The Old Testament anticipates His coming.
  • The Gospels reveal His life and ministry.
  • Acts proclaims His gospel.
  • The Epistles explain His saving work.
  • Revelation reveals His victorious return.

The entire Bible finds its fulfillment in Christ (Luke 24:27; John 5:39).

Christ Is the Head of the Church

The church belongs to Jesus Christ. He alone is:

  • The Head of the Church.
  • The Good Shepherd.
  • The Chief Shepherd.
  • The Cornerstone.
  • The Bridegroom.

Our highest loyalty belongs to Christ—not to any denomination, movement, or theological tradition (Colossians 1:18; Ephesians 1:22–23).

Christ Is Worthy of Our Whole Life

Following Jesus is more than accepting biblical truths. It means:

  • Trusting Him.
  • Loving Him.
  • Obeying Him.
  • Worshiping Him.
  • Serving Him.
  • Proclaiming Him.

The goal of the Christian life is to become more like Christ (Romans 8:29).

Christ Will Return

The risen Lord has promised to come again. When He returns, He will:

  • Judge the world in righteousness.
  • Raise the dead.
  • Defeat evil forever.
  • Establish His everlasting kingdom.
  • Make all things new.

The believer's hope rests in the return of Jesus Christ (Acts 1:11; Revelation 21:1–5).

Christ Alone Is Our Hope

One day every person will stand before God.

On that day our confidence will not rest in:

  • Our good works.
  • Our religious performance.
  • Our knowledge.
  • Our denomination.
  • Our theological system.

Our confidence will rest in Jesus Christ alone. He is:

  • The Lamb of God.
  • The risen Lord.
  • The Savior of the world.
  • The faithful King.
  • The only hope for sinners.

Everyone who comes to Him in faith will be received.

Conclusion

The gospel is all about Jesus Christ.

  • Christ alone became man.
  • Christ alone lived without sin.
  • Christ alone died for our sins.
  • Christ alone rose from the dead.
  • Christ alone reigns as Lord.
  • Christ alone intercedes for His people.
  • Christ alone will return.
  • Christ alone saves.

Everything God has done for our salvation is found in Him.

Therefore, let our confidence never rest in ourselves or in any human system.

Let it rest in Jesus Christ alone—the crucified, risen, reigning, and returning Son of God.

"For from Him and through Him and to Him are all things. To Him be glory forever. Amen." (Romans 11:36)
