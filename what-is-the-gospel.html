The Gospel · I

What Is the Gospel?

The word gospel means "good news." It is the greatest news the world has ever heard: God has acted in history through Jesus Christ to save sinners and reconcile them to Himself.

The gospel is not a philosophy, a moral code, or a theological system. It is the good news of what God has accomplished through the life, death, and resurrection of His Son.

The Gospel in Scripture

The apostle Paul gives one of the clearest summaries of the gospel in 1 Corinthians 15:1–4.

At the heart of the gospel are these historical events:

  • Jesus Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures.
  • He was buried.
  • He rose again on the third day according to the Scriptures.

These events are the foundation of the Christian faith. Christianity is rooted not merely in ideas but in the historical work of Jesus Christ.

Why Do We Need the Gospel?

The Bible teaches that:

  • All people have sinned and fall short of God's glory (Romans 3:23).
  • Sin separates us from God.
  • Humanity cannot save itself.

Yet God, in His great love, did not leave us without hope. He sent His Son, Jesus Christ, to accomplish what we never could.

What Did Christ Accomplish?

Jesus Christ:

  • Lived a perfectly righteous life.
  • Gave Himself as the sacrifice for sin.
  • Died on the cross for our sins.
  • Rose victorious over death.

Through His death and resurrection, God made salvation available to the world.

The gospel proclaims what Christ has accomplished—not what we must accomplish for Him.

How Is Salvation Received?

Salvation is God's gift of grace and is received through faith in Jesus Christ (Ephesians 2:8–9).

The New Testament calls people to:

  • Repent.
  • Believe the gospel.
  • Trust in Jesus Christ alone.
  • Follow Him as Lord.

Saving faith is more than intellectual agreement. It is wholehearted trust in the risen Christ.

The Gospel Is for Everyone

The gospel is proclaimed to all people.

Jesus commanded His followers to:

  • Preach the gospel to the whole world (Mark 16:15).
  • Make disciples of all nations (Matthew 28:18–20).

God sincerely invites everyone to repent and believe.

Everyone who comes to Christ in faith will be received (John 6:37).

The Gospel Changes Lives

Through Christ, believers receive:

  • Forgiveness of sins.
  • New life.
  • Reconciliation with God.
  • Adoption into God's family.
  • The gift of the Holy Spirit.
  • Transformation into the likeness of Christ.

The Christian life does not begin with human achievement but with God's grace.

Conclusion

The gospel is the good news that God has acted in Jesus Christ to save sinners.

The heart of the gospel is simple:

  • Jesus died for our sins.
  • Jesus was buried.
  • Jesus rose again.
  • Salvation is offered by grace through faith.
  • Everyone is called to repent and believe.
  • Everyone who comes to Christ will be received.

Our hope is not found in ourselves or in any theological system, but in Jesus Christ alone, the Savior of the world.
