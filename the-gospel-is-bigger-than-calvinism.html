The Gospel · XI

The Gospel Is Bigger Than Calvinism

Calvinism has influenced Christian theology for centuries and has shaped the thinking of many faithful believers. Yet the gospel did not begin with Calvinism, nor can it be contained within any theological system.

The gospel is God's good news in Jesus Christ. It is greater than every human theological framework because it is centered on a Person—not a system.

The Gospel Came Before Calvinism

The gospel was proclaimed long before theological systems were developed.

The apostles preached:

  • Jesus Christ.
  • His death.
  • His burial.
  • His resurrection.
  • Repentance.
  • Faith.
  • Forgiveness of sins.

Their message was simple: Christ crucified and risen (1 Corinthians 15:1–4; Acts 2:22–39).

The church was founded on the gospel—not on Calvinism, Arminianism, or any later theological tradition.

The Gospel Is About Jesus Christ

The heart of Christianity is not a doctrine but a Savior.

The gospel proclaims that:

  • Jesus became man.
  • Jesus lived without sin.
  • Jesus died for our sins.
  • Jesus rose from the dead.
  • Jesus reigns as Lord.
  • Jesus will return.

Everything in the Christian faith points to Him (Colossians 1:15–20).

The Gospel Is for the Whole World

The New Testament consistently presents the gospel as good news for all people.

Scripture declares that:

  • God loved the world (John 3:16).
  • Christ is the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world (John 1:29).
  • Christ gave Himself as a ransom for all (1 Timothy 2:4–6).
  • God commands all people everywhere to repent (Acts 17:30).

The church therefore proclaims the gospel to every person without distinction.

Theology Should Serve the Gospel

Theology is important. It helps us:

  • Understand Scripture.
  • Protect sound doctrine.
  • Grow in spiritual maturity.
  • Defend the faith.

But theology must always serve the gospel—not replace it.

No theological system should become more important than Jesus Christ or the message of the cross.

The Gospel Creates Disciples

Jesus did not command His followers to make Calvinists.

He commanded them to:

  • Make disciples.
  • Baptize believers.
  • Teach obedience to His commands (Matthew 28:18–20).

The mission of the church is to proclaim Christ and help people follow Him.

Christ Is Greater Than Every System

Every theological tradition seeks to explain biblical truth. Each has strengths. Each has limitations.

Every system must ultimately be tested by Scripture (Acts 17:11).

Our highest authority is not:

  • A confession.
  • A creed.
  • A denomination.
  • A theologian.

Our highest authority is God's Word.

Unity Is Found in Christ

Faithful Christians have disagreed on many theological questions throughout history.

Yet all who trust in Jesus Christ share:

  • One Savior.
  • One Lord.
  • One gospel.
  • One Spirit.
  • One hope (Ephesians 4:4–6).

Our unity is found in Christ rather than complete agreement on every theological issue.

Keep Christ at the Center

Whenever we study theology, we should ask:

  • Does this exalt Jesus Christ?
  • Does it faithfully reflect Scripture?
  • Does it strengthen the proclamation of the gospel?
  • Does it help people know and follow Christ?

Good theology always points beyond itself to Jesus.

Conclusion

The gospel is greater than Calvinism because it is centered on Jesus Christ.

The gospel proclaims:

  • Christ's incarnation.
  • Christ's sinless life.
  • Christ's atoning death.
  • Christ's victorious resurrection.
  • Christ's present reign.
  • Christ's coming return.

Our confidence is not in a theological system. It is not in a human teacher. It is not in a denomination.

Our confidence is in Jesus Christ alone.

The purpose of theology is not simply to understand a system. Its highest purpose is to know Christ, proclaim His gospel, and faithfully follow Him.

"For I decided to know nothing among you except Jesus Christ and Him crucified." (1 Corinthians 2:2)
